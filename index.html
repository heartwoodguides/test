01

Game Overview

Genre & Platform
  • Genre: Massively Multiplayer Online Role-Playing Game (MMORPG)
  • Platform: PC (Windows, macOS, Linux); mobile companion app for inventory & market access
  • Perspective: Fixed isometric camera with optional zoom and rotation
  • Setting: The vast fantasy world of Elderstone — distinct biomes, cities, and ancient ruins
Core Pillars
  • Skill Mastery — 28 interlocking skills reward long-term progression without hard gates
  • Player Economy — All tradeable items flow through a live player market; the game never competes with players
  • Fair Play — Bonds eliminate gold-selling by letting players exchange premium currency for in-game gold
  • Consent-Based PvP — No forced PvP. Competitive play is always opt-in and richly rewarded
  • Enduring World — Seasonal content, world events, and player-built structures keep the world alive for years
02

Skills System

Elderstone features 28 skills split across four categories. Every skill runs from level 1 to 120, with a visible cap of 99 and a hidden mastery tier above it that unlocks prestige cosmetics and minor passive bonuses. No skill is mandatory — players are free to focus on whatever they enjoy.

Skill Progression
  • Experience Curve — XP requirements roughly double every 7 levels, keeping early progress fast and endgame meaningful
  • Milestone Rewards — Every 10 levels grants a cosmetic title, emote, or passive bonus (e.g. Mining 50 = +5% ore chance)
  • Level 99 Cape — Reaching 99 in any skill awards a Skill Cape with a unique animation and passive perk
  • Level 120 Mastery — Unlocks a trimmed cape variant and one powerful passive unique to that skill
  • Total Level — Sum of all 28 skill levels; displayed on the player profile and used for leaderboard ranking

Skill Interactions

Skills are deliberately interlocked so that specialisation is valuable but no single player can be entirely self-sufficient without significant investment.

  • Mining + Smithing — Mine ore, smelt bars, forge gear — the classic production chain
  • Farming + Herblore — Grow herbs on patches, brew potions for combat or sale
  • Woodcutting + Fletching + Ranged — Chop logs, craft bows and arrows, equip and use them
  • Slayer + Combat — Slayer tasks send players to specific monsters; high-level tasks need high combat
  • Dungeoneering + All Skills — Floor difficulty scales with the party's average total level; every skill has uses inside
  • Cartography + Agility — Higher Agility unlocks shortcuts; Cartography reveals where those shortcuts lead
03

Combat System

Combat is real-time with a one-tick action queue — players can queue one action ahead (attack, special, eat food, switch prayer), giving skilled players a meaningful edge without making combat inaccessible to newcomers.

Combat Styles
  • Melee — Three sub-styles (Slash, Stab, Crush), each strong against different enemy armour types
  • Ranged — Long range, lower defence requirement; arrows and bolts carry varying special effects
  • Magic — Elemental, Ancient, and Lunar spellbooks; can autocast or be manually triggered
  • Hybrid — Players can switch styles mid-fight; gear switching is a high skill-ceiling activity
The Prayer System

Prayer points are spent to activate temporary buffs — attack boosts, protection prayers that reduce incoming damage by 50%, or utility prayers like eagle eye and rapid restore. Points restore at altars, via buried bones, or through Prayer potions.

Summoning in Combat

Spirit familiars can be summoned using crafted pouches. Combat familiars deal supplemental damage or provide special scroll abilities. Non-combat familiars carry extra inventory space, gather resources passively, or provide skill boosts.

04

Optional PvP

PvP is never forced. Players will never lose items to PvP without having actively chosen to enter a PvP zone or game mode. The border of every PvP area shows a clear warning prompt.
The Wilderness

The Wilderness is a large northern region of the world map. Entering it is a deliberate choice — a warning prompt appears at the border.

  • Risk vs Reward — The deepest Wilderness areas contain the rarest resource spawns, dungeons, and boss encounters
  • Item Protection — By default, players protect their 3 most valuable items on death; a skull mechanic removes this protection for aggressors
  • Level Brackets — PvP zones are divided by combat level range (50–70, 70–100, 100+) to prevent unfair predation
  • Escape Mechanics — Teleport spells work up to level 30 Wilderness; beyond that, agility shortcuts and terrain knowledge are your tools
Opt-In Arenas
⚔ Duel Arena
1v1 wagers with custom rule sets — no prayer, no potions, specific weapon styles. Fully consensual risk.
🏰 Castle Wars
Team-based capture-the-flag in a dedicated instanced arena. Cosmetic rewards only — no economy impact.
⚡ Soul Wars
Large-scale team PvP combined with PvE. Resource gathering mid-fight creates deep strategic choices.
🏆 Ranked Duels
A seasonal ELO ladder for 1v1 combat. Top players earn prestigious cosmetics and profile badges.
PvP Rewards

All PvP rewards are cosmetic or convenience-based — never pay-to-win or stat-dominant. This keeps PvP desirable without making it mandatory for PvE progression. Blood Shards drop in the Wilderness and are exchanged for exclusive cosmetics at the Wilderness Exchange.

05

Player Economy & Market

The economy is entirely player-driven. The game does not sell items obtainable through play. NPC shops exist only for base-tier commodities (empty vials, basic tools) and act as a price floor, never a ceiling.

The Grand Exchange

The Grand Exchange (GE) is a centralised asynchronous marketplace. Players list buy and sell orders and the system matches them automatically — no need to stand in a trading hub.

  • Live Pricing — Price history graphs are available in-game and via a public API for third-party tools
  • Volume Limits — Each item has a 4-hour buy limit to prevent market cornering; limits are generous for common items
  • Offline Trading — Orders execute while offline; items and coin wait in the GE collection box
  • Market Alerts — Set price targets and receive a notification when they are reached
Direct Trade & Player Shops
  • Direct Trade — Face-to-face trade windows with a double-confirm screen and GE reference prices to prevent scamming
  • Player-Owned Shops — Construction level 60 unlocks a personal shop visible on the world map; a 1% listing fee maintains balance
Gold Sinks

A healthy economy requires robust gold sinks to counteract inflation from monster drops.

  • Death costs — Retrieving items after a PvE death incurs a small fee (waived for Ironman accounts)
  • Skill training — Many artisan skills consume materials that must be bought or gathered
  • Construction — Building and furnishing a house is expensive, entirely cosmetic — a pure prestige sink
  • Cosmetic upgrades — Recolours, transmog tokens, and effect unlocks are purchased with gold at in-game vendors
  • GE transaction tax — A 0.5% fee on all GE trades is burned from the economy permanently
06

Bonds

Bonds are the primary mechanism for ensuring Elderstone remains free of illicit real-money trading (RMT) while giving both paying and free players a fair path forward.
What Is a Bond?

A Bond is an in-game item that can be purchased from the official store with real money, or bought from other players on the Grand Exchange for in-game gold. Once used, a Bond grants 14 days of Premium Membership.

  • Legal alternative — No need to visit a shady website. The official route is competitive and safe
  • Seller protection — Players selling Bonds for gold are conducting a fully sanctioned transaction — no ban risk
  • Price discovery — The GE Bond price reflects the true real-money-to-gold rate; publicly visible and stable
  • No middlemen — The official Bond rate undercuts black market sellers, collapsing the incentive to use them
Bond Mechanics
  • Untradeable after use — Bonds become untradeable once opened, preventing secondary resale fraud
  • Tradeable format — An unactivated Bond can be gifted or listed on the GE
  • Partial use — Bonds can pay for individual premium services (extra bank tabs, cosmetic slots) at a prorated cost
  • No P2W — Bonds only grant Membership and cosmetic access. Never combat stats, XP boosts, or best-in-slot gear
Premium Membership Benefits
  • Extra skills — Members unlock the full 28-skill roster; free players access 14 core skills
  • Members worlds — Lower-population, faster-respawn servers
  • Expanded bank — Free: 200 slots. Members: 800 slots, with further tabs purchasable via Bonds
  • More quests — Main quest line is free; member quests offer the richest story and unique rewards
  • Cosmetics wardrobe — Members store and swap cosmetic overrides without using bank space

World Design

Regions
Ashenvale Plains
The starting region — gentle hills, starter quests, and the main city of Silvergate. Welcoming and safe.
The Ironholds
Volcanic mountain range rich in rare ores; home to the Dwarven trade alliance and Master Smithing trainers.
Mirewood Marsh
Dense, dangerous swamp full of high-level Slayer creatures and rare Herblore herbs. Hostile terrain rewards preparation.
Crystalspire Coast
Coastal cliffs and sea caves; prime Fishing, Hunter, and high-level Agility training. Shipwreck dungeons offshore.
The Deep Wilds
The Wilderness — northern tundra scarred by ancient magic. PvP-enabled. Highest risk, highest reward in the game.
Underhollow
A vast cave network for Dungeoneering — procedurally generated co-op floors. Every run is different.
World Events
  • Seasonal Festivals — Four per year tied to the in-game calendar; unique cosmetics, skill boosts, and event quests
  • Invasions — Random enemy waves attack cities; players band together to defend, earning event currency
  • World Bosses — Rare server-wide boss spawns announced in advance; require mass co-ordination and reward unique drops
  • Player Milestones — When the server collectively reaches a skill milestone, a world bonus activates for all players
Player Housing

Player-Owned Houses (Construction skill) are personal instanced spaces. Players build and furnish rooms that provide functional benefits — altars, forges, dungeon portals — and cosmetic expression. Houses can be set to public, friends-only, or private.

08

Quests & Narrative

Quests are the primary vehicle for world-building, lore, and unique rewards. They are never required for skill training but always offer the best introduction to content areas.

  • Novice Quests — Short, accessible; teach core systems; reward starter gear and XP
  • Journeyman Quests — Multi-stage; involve multiple skills and areas; deepen the world's lore
  • Master Quests — Rare, long-form stories requiring high skill levels; reward unique items and unlock endgame areas
  • World Quests — Server-wide story arcs that unfold over a season; outcomes shaped by collective player choices
09

Monetisation

Every item that affects gameplay must be earnable in-game. Real money buys convenience and cosmetics only.
What We Sell
  • Bonds (Premium Membership)
  • Cosmetic outfits & pets
  • Mount skins & particle effects
  • Name changes
  • Bank tab expansions (via Bonds only)
Never Sold
  • XP boosts of any kind
  • Stat-bearing gear or weapons
  • Drop rate modifiers
  • Pay-to-win combat advantages
  • Anything only obtainable with cash
10

Anti-Cheat & Fair Play

  • Botting Detection — Behavioural telemetry flags inhuman click patterns and repetitive action sequences; flagged accounts are investigated before action is taken
  • Gold Seller Interdiction — Suspicious bulk gold transfers between unlinked accounts are rate-limited and reviewed
  • Ironman Mode — Optional self-sufficiency mode that disables GE access and direct trade; for players who want a pure solo economy
  • Hardcore Ironman — Ironman with permanent death; a separate, respected leaderboard
  • Appeals Process — Every ban can be appealed through a transparent ticket system with a human review stage
Accessibility
  • Colourblind Modes — Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia presets for all UI elements
  • Input Remapping — Full keyboard and controller remapping; mouse-only and keyboard-only play both supported
  • UI Scaling — All UI elements scale independently from 75% to 200%
  • Screen Reader — Chatbox, GE prices, and skill levels output to system accessibility APIs
  • Subtitles — All voiced dialogue and tutorial narration has full subtitles

Appendix: Key Design Decisions

Why Opt-In PvP?

Forced open-world PvP creates a hostile new-player experience and drives away a large segment of the potential audience. By confining PvP to the Wilderness (consent-at-the-border) and dedicated arenas, we preserve the thrill of player-versus-player combat while guaranteeing that every other player in the world is a collaborator, not a threat. The rewards inside PvP zones are sufficient to make it highly compelling for those who seek it.

Why Bonds Instead of a Direct Gold Shop?

Selling gold directly creates an infinite inflation vector and removes the need for gathering skills. Bonds instead monetise time — a player with less time can convert real money to membership or gold via the Bond market, while a player with more time can convert gameplay effort into membership by selling Bonds they earned through play. Both players are served; the economy remains player-driven.

Why 28 Skills?

28 skills provides a vast progression space without redundancy. Each skill occupies a unique mechanical niche. The breadth ensures that every playstyle — combat specialist, master crafter, explorer, social trader — has deep and rewarding progression. A player who never swings a sword can still reach an impressive total level and earn genuine respect on the leaderboards.
